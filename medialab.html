MATERIAL I/O
MIT MEDIA LAB

I/O MATERIAL is our statement of future computing. We call ourselves "atom hackers". When computing jumps out of computer screens and moves into physical environment, it's next home is matter. By designing material across nano, micro and macro scales, by integrating material live or dead, and by engineering material sensing and representing, we are creating a future with responsive furnitures, living rooms, vehicles and air planes.

Through this site, we hope to share our projects, methods and philosophy with you.

This is a part of a bigger effort of pursuing physical manifestation of digital information at Tangible Media Group, MIT Media Lab. We call this vision Radical Atom.
